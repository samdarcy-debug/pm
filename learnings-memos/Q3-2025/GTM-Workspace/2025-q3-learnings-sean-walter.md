## **Key Learnings**



1.  **New competition**: While we are seeing consolidation in Sales-Tech

    our competition has stiffened. We are no longer just competing with

    Sales Intelligence tools - we are competing with Notion, ChatGPT. IF

    our app isn't truly best in class, there's less reason for users to

    bother with the application. While we as an org are hedging our bets

    with ZI data as a source for LLMs, integrations - this is an

    existential risk for our application. If it's not as frictionless

    and fit for purpose for frontline sellers - it need not exists.



2.  **Trust through explainability:** Users are wary of GenAI

    application's accuracy. Every claim must be easily cited to build

    trust. Even for internal users, if we return incorrect responses a

    few times, rebuilding trust is difficult.



3.  **Network Effects and Sharing:** To fully capitalize on the GenAI

    shift, we must build a platform that thrives on user collaboration

    and shared innovation. By fostering strong network effects, similar

    to what companies like Clay have achieved, we can establish

    Workspace as the indispensable platform for the GenAI era. This

    means prioritizing features that encourage sharing, community

    engagement, and the co-creation of valuable assets within the

    platform first at a tenant level, then ultimately cross-tenant.
