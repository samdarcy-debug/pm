# **GTM Studio Roundup - 08/04/2025**

**GTM Plays M1 PRD is 70% complete and scheduled for critical
stakeholder review Thursday**, positioning us to enter build stage if
approved. However, **dependency mapping reveals significant cross-team
coordination needs** that could impact M1 timeline. Meanwhile,
Jagannath\'s AI Agent capabilities achieved breakthrough progress with
multi-column enrichment and custom signals integration, and Arun\'s FAQ
Agent for ROI dashboard addresses the overwhelming complexity of 20+
metrics. Critical path items need immediate attention: Hubspot
integration clarity for ROI GA and DeltaX contract negotiations to
prevent vendor lock-in risks.

with Jagannath\'s team completing multi-column enrichment including Data

aggregates, scoops). The conversational experience now supports workbook

decisions finalized for temporary playground functionality versus

Veronica\'s team achieved 70% completion on the M1 PRD, with focused

Dependencies are significant, requiring comprehensive mapping to ensure
M1 delivery success.

FAQ Agent implementation** as Arun\'s team addressed the overwhelming
nature of 20+ metrics through embedded AI assistance. This self-service
approach reduces support dependency while empowering customer
understanding of complex analytics, critical for GA readiness.

### **Strategic Challenges**

**Hubspot integration uncertainty threatens ROI GA timeline and affects
approximately half our customer base** who have connected CRM with
opportunity data sharing. Root cause analysis reveals integration
platform data quality issues requiring immediate decision: proceed with
GTM Data Management temporary solution or punt post-GA. This impacts
significant revenue potential given Hubspot\'s prevalence in our
customer base and renewal implications.

**Cross-team dependency complexity for GTM Plays M1 is significant**.
Requirements gathering has uncovered extensive coordination needs with
workflows team, individual action/trigger owners, and platform teams.
While not blocking current progress, the dependency matrix suggests
implementation complexity that requires proactive management to prevent
M1 timeline risks and resource bottlenecks.

**Vendor relationship management presents strategic business continuity
risks** as DeltaX demonstrated with production incident caused by
unrestricted access to demand gen account. Though migration solutions
progressed well, long-term strategy must address either locked-in longer
contracts (3+ years) or direct LinkedIn API access development to
eliminate vendor dependency vulnerabilities and price manipulation
risks.

## **Strategic Insights**

### **Key Learnings & Discoveries**

**Technical architecture decisions around AI user experience revealed
the magic versus control paradox** as teams across Data Management,
Workbooks, and Plays consistently discovered that customers demand
transparency and intervention capabilities over autonomous AI actions.
This pattern emerged from Data Management stakeholder feedback
emphasizing control over configuration changes, workbook agent feedback
requiring human-in-the-loop functionality, and plays orchestration
needing user oversight of automation decisions.

**Workflow system limitations surfaced as fundamental constraint for
activation capabilities** when Mohan\'s team discovered that current
workbook export V1 workflows cannot support essential sales engagement
features like add-to-sequence, add-to-campaign, or cadence management
out-of-the-box. This technical gap requires additional orchestration
capability development and impacts all downstream activation pathways
including Outreach, Salesloft, and other sales tools integration.

**Customer onboarding experience gaps became apparent through early
access program feedback** as technical complexity around workbook
creation, signal understanding, and feature adoption created barriers
despite strong underlying capabilities. This drove team prioritization
toward conversational interfaces and simplified user journeys, with
Jagannath\'s agent work directly addressing the need for natural
language interaction over technical configuration requirements.

### **Cross-Team Dependencies**

Our work with **Carlos and the Platform team on GTM Config context and
facets** continues to be critical for both plays functionality and
Copilot integration. Progress made this week with base use case
alignment around product offering context definition, though engineering
sizing and directional timelines still pending. **Workflows team
coordination** remains essential for both plays trigger/action
implementation and workbook activation capabilities, with Marc Frail\'s
continued collaboration required for comprehensive trigger library
development.

**Integration Platform team partnership with Erica and Prateek**
requires immediate attention for Hubspot ROI resolution, while
**Analytics team alignment** progresses smoothly for usage definition
format and business question prioritization across multiple workstreams.

## **Looking Ahead**

**Primary focus centers on Thursday\'s GTM Plays M1 PRD review** which
represents our critical gate to build stage entry and H2 execution
momentum. Success requires comprehensive dependency documentation,
stakeholder alignment, and clear risk mitigation strategies for

**Technical implementation acceleration** will proceed across multiple

**Strategic decision points require executive attention on vendor
