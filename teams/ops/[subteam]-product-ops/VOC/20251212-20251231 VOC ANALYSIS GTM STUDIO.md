### ‚ùå WHAT'S NOT WORKING (Ranked by Frequency)

#### RANK \#1: User Adoption & Daily Workflow Concerns

Raised in 57 of 62 calls (92%)

User adoption concerns affect nine out of ten calls, making this the overwhelming dominant friction point. Customers consistently express skepticism about whether sellers would actually use Studio or whether it would become "shelfware." Concerns center on whether the tool would fit into existing daily workflows.

The objection manifests as operations/RevOps buyers who understand Studio's value but question whether sellers will adopt it. This is a "buyer-user mismatch" where the economic buyer sees clear ROI but doesn't believe end users will change behavior.

Key objection patterns: ‚Ä¢ "Will sellers actually use this?" \- repeated in nearly every call ‚Ä¢ RevOps persona understands value but questions whether sellers will adopt ‚Ä¢ Concerns about training burden and change management complexity ‚Ä¢ Fear of tool becoming shelfware despite clear capabilities ‚Ä¢ Need for seamless workflow integration vs. separate destination ‚Ä¢ Requires executive mandate for adoption vs. organic usage

Example from Verkada: Mallory questioned whether reps would actually log into Studio daily given they already work across Outreach, Tien, and direct email. She stated that adding another platform \- even one with clear value \- creates adoption risk if it's not seamlessly embedded in their current workflow. The "will sellers actually use this?" question dominated the objection discussion.

Pattern across 57 accounts: This objection appeared in 92% of calls across all customer segments, company sizes, and industries. The consistency indicates this is a fundamental positioning challenge rather than a product gap. The concern exists independently of feature completeness \- customers question adoption even when all their technical requirements are met.

#### RANK \#2: Missing Features & Product Gaps

Raised in 52 of 62 calls (84%) **Trend:** ‚Üë Increasing from 32% last period

Missing features affect 84% of calls, TRIPLING from last period (32% ‚Üí 84%). Customers identify specific capabilities they expected or needed that Studio doesn't currently offer. These gaps range from missing integrations to workflow features to data types.

Critically, these aren't edge cases \- they're core requirements that customers articulate as blockers to full deployment. The dramatic increase suggests product-market fit challenges are growing as Studio matures and encounters more sophisticated buyer requirements.

Most common missing features (detailed breakdown in Section 5): ‚Ä¢ Conversation intelligence integration (Gong, Chorus, Clari) \- 36 accounts ‚Ä¢ Clay-style visual workflow builder with custom formulas \- 21 accounts ‚Ä¢ Native CRM integrations beyond Salesforce \- 11 accounts ‚Ä¢ Sales engagement platform integration (Outreach, Salesloft) \- 20 accounts ‚Ä¢ Intent data platform integration (6sense, Demandbase) \- 11 accounts

Example from Merkle: Merkle identified critical gaps including lack of native HubSpot integration (they're a HubSpot shop, not Salesforce), inability to customize AI prompts for their specific industry vertical, and missing support for their preferred data warehouse solution. These weren't edge cases but core requirements for their evaluation.

Pattern across 52 accounts: Missing integrations (HubSpot, specific CRMs, data warehouses), lack of industry-specific templates, insufficient technographic granularity, limited custom field support, missing API endpoints, incomplete reporting capabilities
