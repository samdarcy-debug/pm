SECTION 1: WHAT WE LEARNED
✅ WHAT'S RESONATING
AI-Powered Intelligence & Automation

The AI-powered capabilities of GTM Workspace consistently resonated across 17 accounts. Customers responded positively to seeing how AI generates account insights, drafts personalized outreach, and provides next best action recommendations. The automation of research and content creation particularly addressed pain points around sellers spending too much time on manual administrative work.

Example from Smartsheet Inc. (https://chorus.ai/meeting/83BAF7F95AB84FFCBE9E3EC9BFE0215E): "a single pane of glass, tying together disparate data sources and providing AI-powered next best actions,"

Also seen with: WTW, Zafran, Aggreko, Perficient, Allego Inc. and 11 more accounts

Single Pane of Glass for Seller Workflows

Nine accounts resonated with the concept of consolidating multiple tools into one workspace. Customers articulated frustration with sellers jumping between Salesforce, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, ZoomInfo, and various other tools. The vision of a unified interface where sellers can research accounts, access signals, and execute outreach without context-switching generated positive reactions.

Example from Smartsheet Inc. (https://chorus.ai/meeting/83BAF7F95AB84FFCBE9E3EC9BFE0215E): "jumping between multiple tools like Salesloft, ZoomInfo, LinkedIn Sales Navigator, and then manually aggregating everything in ChatGPT or notebooks,"

Also seen with: TrueContext, Datarobot, Inc., ValueBlue, enVista Llc. and 4 more accounts

Signals & Intent Data for Prioritization

Thirteen accounts showed strong interest in leveraging buying signals and intent data to help sellers prioritize their outreach. The ability to surface accounts showing active interest through job postings, funding events, technology changes, or other triggers resonated as a way to make sellers more efficient and improve conversion rates by focusing on warm opportunities.

Example from Smartsheet Inc. (https://chorus.ai/meeting/83BAF7F95AB84FFCBE9E3EC9BFE0215E): Customer recognized value in signal-based prioritization to help sellers focus on high-intent accounts.

Also seen with: WTW, Zafran, Fraxion, SmartLinx Solutions, Datarobot, Inc. and 7 more accounts

Workflow Automation & Time Savings

Seventeen accounts resonated with the workflow automation capabilities that reduce manual tasks for sellers. The ability to automate research, generate meeting prep materials, draft emails, and streamline CRM updates appealed to customers looking to give their reps more selling time and reduce administrative burden.

Example from WTW (https://chorus.ai/meeting/3603DFAC44744C8D8885090DA0E148C6): Customer showed enthusiasm for automation capabilities that would save sellers time on repetitive tasks.

Also seen with: Zafran, Aggreko, Perficient, Fraxion, Allego Inc. and 11 more accounts
❌ WHAT'S NOT WORKING
Unclear Value Proposition vs Existing Tools

Five accounts struggled to understand how Workspace differs from their current toolset or what specific problems it solves that existing tools don't address. Customers expressed confusion about whether Workspace is a CRM replacement, a data enrichment tool, or something else entirely. The positioning needs clearer differentiation and concrete use cases that show unique value.

Example from Aggreko (https://chorus.ai/meeting/69A1D6B9F195490C9F46B2C6CD27A485): "I don't know if you put Aggreko ZoomInfo users into different buckets"

Pattern across 5 accounts: Aggreko, Allego Inc., Bluegrass Supply Chain Services Llc., Cvent, Kayak

Competitive Differentiation Not Compelling

Ten accounts questioned how Workspace differs from competitive tools they're already evaluating or using. Comparisons to Clay, Apollo, Gong, and LinkedIn Sales Navigator came up repeatedly, with customers unclear on why they should choose Workspace over these alternatives. The differentiation messaging needs to be more concrete and specific about unique capabilities.

Example from Aggreko (https://chorus.ai/meeting/69A1D6B9F195490C9F46B2C6CD27A485): "I don't know if you put Aggreko ZoomInfo users into different buckets"

Pattern across 10 accounts: Aggreko, TrueContext, Datarobot, Inc., LOCALiQ UK, Domino, People Corp. and 4 more

Demo Pacing & Feature Overload

Ten accounts indicated the demo moved too quickly or showed too many features without clear connection to customer pain points. Sellers struggled to follow along or understand which capabilities were most relevant to their specific use cases. Demos need better pacing and more focus on 2-3 key workflows that solve customer-specific problems.

Example from Zafran (https://chorus.ai/meeting/E5B50FC86D6048538FB6A3233BC6F74E): Customer had difficulty following the demo and connecting features to their specific needs.

Pattern across 10 accounts: Zafran, Bluegrass Supply Chain Services Llc., ValueBlue, Addison Lee, Nidec and 5 more

SECTION 4: PRODUCT FEEDBACK
Theme #1: Integration & Connectivity (18 accounts requested)
What customers want: Broader integration support with sales engagement platforms, marketing automation tools, conversation intelligence systems, and specialized CRMs. Customers want seamless data flow between Workspace and their existing tech stack without manual data entry or export/import processes.

Specific requests:

Outreach and Salesloft integration - Smartsheet Inc., Zafran, Perficient
Gong and Chorus integration for call insights - Datarobot, Inc., Citizens Financial Group
Marketo and Pardot connectivity - Smartsheet Inc., Perficient
Microsoft Dynamics CRM support - Nidec, ValueBlue
Custom API endpoints for proprietary systems - Avis Budget Group, Inc., WTW

Priority: High Rationale: Integration concerns came up in 19 calls as objections and 17 as questions. This is actively blocking deals where customers can't see clear path to connect Workspace with critical tools. Without broader integration support, Workspace remains siloed and doesn't deliver on single-pane-of-glass promise.
Theme #2: Workflow Automation & Customization (23 accounts requested)
What customers want: More flexible workflow automation that adapts to their specific sales processes. Customers want ability to customize AI prompts, create company-specific templates, define custom triggers for outreach, and automate handoffs between different stages of the sales cycle.

Specific requests:

Custom AI prompt templates for industry-specific messaging - Datarobot, Inc., Zafran, Payscale
Automated task creation based on account signals - Kayak, Darktrace, Unmind Ltd.
Customizable workflow stages matching their sales process - Citizens Financial Group, Cvent
Automated sequence enrollment from Workspace - Allego Inc., Fraxion, SmartLinx Solutions
Multi-touch campaign orchestration - High Point Networks Llc., Perficient

Priority: High Rationale: Workflow customization was the most frequently requested capability across 23 accounts. Customers see potential in Workspace but need it to match their specific processes rather than forcing them into a generic workflow. This flexibility could be key differentiator vs competitors.
Theme #3: UI Customization & Personalization (11 accounts requested)
What customers want: Ability to customize the Workspace interface for different sales roles, regions, or product lines. Customers want sellers to see only relevant data and actions based on their specific territory, product focus, or seniority level.

Specific requests:

Role-based views for SDRs vs AEs vs CSMs - Smartsheet Inc., WTW, Aggreko
Regional customization for different geographies - Nidec, LOCALiQ UK, Addison Lee
Product-line specific workspaces - Perficient, Datarobot, Inc.
Custom data fields and layouts - Cvent, iVision

Priority: Medium Rationale: While important for enterprise customers with complex sales organizations, this is a nice-to-have rather than a deal blocker. Most customers can work within standard interface if core functionality and integrations are strong.
Theme #4: Reporting & Analytics (2 accounts requested)
What customers want: Better reporting on seller adoption, activity metrics, and ROI from using Workspace. Customers want to track which sellers are using the tool, what actions they're taking, and how it impacts pipeline and conversion rates.

Specific requests:

Dashboard showing seller adoption metrics - Pacific Office Automation
ROI reporting connecting Workspace usage to pipeline - WTW

Priority: Evaluate Rationale: Only 2 accounts raised this explicitly, suggesting it's not a widespread need. May become more important post-implementation when customers want to measure impact, but not currently blocking initial deals.
